##  Seção 14.3 Arpeggiated Accompaniments

###  Subsection 14.3.1 Arpeggios

One way to express acordes rhythmically is through arpeggios in one part and a bass line in octaves in a inferior part, as in the following example from Beethoven’s Moonlight Sonata. 

![](external/images/unit3/texture-arpeggios-moonlight.svg)

Figura 14.3.1. Ludwig van Beethoven, Moonlight Sonata, Op. 27, No. 2, I (1802)

The next example has descending arpeggios. 

![](external/images/unit3/texture-arpeggios-alicia-keys.svg)

Figura 14.3.2. Alicia Keys, “If I Ain’t Got You” (2004)

Notice that in both the Beethoven and Alicia Keys examples there is the harmonious intervalo of a tenth (an oitava plus a third) between the bass (lowest) voice and the soprano (highest) voice. 

The following examples have arpeggios that ascend and descend through a acorde. 

![](external/images/unit3/texture-arpeggios-colour.svg)

Figura 14.3.3. James Pankow, “Colour My World” (1970)

![](external/images/unit3/texture-arpeggios-rihanna.svg)

Figura 14.3.4. Fred Ball, Joseph Angel, and Robyn Fenty, “Love on the Brain” (2016)

Notice in the above example that there is also an organ playing block acordes to create a sense of legato in the texture. 

The next two examples are from more recent popular music. 

![](external/images/unit3/textures-alberti-adele.svg)

Figura 14.3.5. Adele Adkins and Dan Wilson, “Someone Like You” (2011)

![](external/images/unit3/texture-alberti-one-republic.svg)

Figura 14.3.6. Ryan Tedder, “Secrets” (2009)

Below is an example in \\(\begin{smallmatrix}4\\\4\end{smallmatrix}\\) with arpeggios in sixteenth notas. 

![](external/images/unit3/texture-arpeggios-no-scrubs.svg)

Figura 14.3.7. Kevin Briggs, Kandi Burress, Tameka Cottle, Lisa Lopes, “No Scrubs” (1999)

###  Subsection 14.3.2 Alberti Bass

Alberti bass accompaniment padrões involve arpeggios that do not arpeggiate acordes in a simple upward or downward motion, but in a “low–high–middle–high” padrão as you can see in the examples below. 

![](external/images/unit3/texture-alberti-k545.svg)

Figura 14.3.8. Mozart, Piano Sonata K. 545, I (1788)

The next example uses the same Alberti padrão as in the Mozart example above, but transposed to E minor and in a inferior register. 

![](external/images/unit3/texture-alberti-pipeline.svg)

Figura 14.3.9. Brian Carman and Bob Spickard, “Pipeline” (1962)
