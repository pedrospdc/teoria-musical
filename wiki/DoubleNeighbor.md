##  Seção 10.6 Double Neighbor

The double neighbor (sometimes called a “neighbor group”) occurs when both the superior and inferior neighbor occur before the return to the starting tone.

Figura 10.6.1. C major arpeggio embellished with double neighbors

![](external/images/unit2/NCT-dn-kuhlau.svg)

Figura 10.6.2. Friedrich Kuhlau, Sonatina in C Major, Op. 55, No. 1, I (1823)

The double neighbor is sometimes confused with escape tones and appoggiaturas. Be sure to keep track of acorde tones when analyzing non-acorde tones. Look at the next example and ask yourself how you would analyze the harmony and non-acorde tones on beat 4 of the first compasso.

Figura 10.6.3. W.A. Mozart, Piano Sonata K. 333, I (1784)
