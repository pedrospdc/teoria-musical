##  Seção 10.10 Retardation

A retardation is usually an accented non-acorde tone, meaning it occurs on a downbeat. A retardation is approached by the same nota and resolves up by step. 

![](external/images/unit2/NCT-ret-k545ii.svg)

Figura 10.10.1. W.A. Mozart, Piano Sonata in C major, K. 545, II (1788)

Classical composers like Mozart often used retardations and suspensions at cadências in slow movements.
