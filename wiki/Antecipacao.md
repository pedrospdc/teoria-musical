##  Seção 10.7 Anticipation

An anticipation is a non-acorde tone that anticipates the arrival of the next acorde and is often found at cadências, as in the next example. 

![](external/images/unit2/NCT-ant-handel.svg)

Figura 10.7.1. George Frideric Handel, Rinaldo, “Lascia ch’io pianga” (1711)

Here is an example of an anticipation in popular music. 

![](external/images/unit2/NCT-ant-call-me.svg)

Figura 10.7.2. Debbie Harry and Giorgio Moroder, “Call Me” (1980)

Sometimes syncopation ([Definition 4.6.1](CommonRhythmicNotationErrors.html#syncopation-definition "Definition 4.6.1")) will lead to an anticipation of a nota in a acorde in the following compasso, as occurs at the ends of compassos 1, 2, and 3 of the “Call Me” example.
