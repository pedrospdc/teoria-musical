Figura 24.9.4. Mozart, Allegro in B-bemol major

On scratch paper, create a diagram of the form. Include section labels using uppercase letters, phrase labels using lowercase letters, and cadências using the abbreviations PAC, IAC, HC, DC, PC. 

Circle all of the terms that apply to the name of the form: 

![](external/images/unit7/PRAC-EXER-bin-tern-terms-to-circle.svg)

Answer.

![](external/images/unit7/PRAC-EXER-mozart-allegro-DIAGRAM-KEY.svg)

Two-Reprise Continuous Ternary, if looking at the proportion, or Two-Reprise Continuous Rounded Binary, if you interpret the first two notas in compasso 13 as being built from the first two notas in compasso 1—and if you are considering that Mozart, as a Classical-era composer, is likely to compose a rounded binary. Two-Reprise Continuous Balanced Binary is a label that describes that the endings of the halves are the same, but doesn’t take into account the return of the opening material in compasso 21.
