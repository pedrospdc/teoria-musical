### Exercise Group.

Jazz Theory: Day Three 

#### 3.

Voice lead the following progressions, maintaining five voices throughout. Remember to spell notas enharmonically when necessary. In the first example, use “close” voicing, keeping all the superior notas as close together as possible. In the second example, use Root–3rd–7th or Root–7th–3rd always for the lowest three voices, then realize the rest of the lead sheet symbol with the superior two parts (“spread” voicing). Use Root–3rd–6th for 6th acordes and Root–4th–7th for sus acordes. You may need to omit the 5th to maintain five parts throughout.
