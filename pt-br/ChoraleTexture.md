##  Seção 14.2 Chorale Texture

Chorale textures are those in which there is a acorde for every (or nearly every) melody nota. A familiar example of chorale texture is the “Star-Spangled Banner.” 

![](external/images/unit3/textures-chorale-star-spangled.svg)

Figura 14.2.1. Francis Scott Key and John Stafford Smith, “The Star-Spangled Banner” (1814)

In the following example by Schubert, the melody at times moves in a slightly different ritmo that the acordes below. 

![](external/images/unit3/texture-chorale-nebensonnen.svg)

Figura 14.2.2. Franz Schubert, Winterreise, D.911, “Die Nebensonnen” (1828)

Chorale textures are also described as “homorhythmic” because all of the parts move in the same ritmo. 

![](external/images/unit3/texture-chorale-some-nights.svg)

Figura 14.2.3. Jeff Bhasker, Nate Ruess, Andrew Dost, Jack Antonoff, “Some Nights” (2011)
