##  Seção 16.4 Other Occurrences of Six-Four Acordes

Second-inversion acordes with the fifth of the acorde in the bass, also known as \\(\left.\text{}^{6}_{4}\right.\\) acordes, are special acordes found only in four situations in classical music. 

  1. Cadential six-four (discussed in the previous section) 

  2. Passing six-four: the bass line will look like a passing-tone figure, with three stepwise ascending or descending notas, and the middle acorde will be a \\(\left.\text{}^{6}_{4}\right.\\) acorde 

![](external/images/unit4/figured-bass-passing-64.svg)

  3. Pedal six-four: the bassline will consist of three repeated notas, and the middle acorde will be a \\(\left.\text{}^{6}_{4}\right.\\) acorde. 

![](external/images/unit4/figured-bass-pedal-64.svg)

  4. Melodic bass six-four: in this situation, the bass voice has the melody; when the fifth of the acorde occurs in this bass voice melody, the result will be a \\(\left.\text{}^{6}_{4}\right.\\) acorde.
