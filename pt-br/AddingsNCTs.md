##  Seção 28.3 Adding Non-Acorde Tones to a Acorde Progressão

Here are ideas for how to add non-acorde tones (or “embellishments”) to one of the voice parts of an SATB acorde progressão. 

  1. Repeated nota: add superior or inferior neighbor 

![](external/images/unit9/vl-nct-adding-nct-repeated-nota.svg)

Figura 28.3.1.
  2. Step down:

     1. Add a [suspension](Suspension.html#sus-all-numbers "Figura 10.9.2") by delaying the resolution of the nota 

![](external/images/unit9/vl-nct-adding-nct-step-down-sus.svg)

Figura 28.3.2.
     2. Add an escape tone by moving by step in the opposite direction of the original stepwise movement, then leap 

![](external/images/unit9/vl-nct-adding-nct-step-down-esc.svg)

Figura 28.3.3.
     3. Add a double neighbor 

![](external/images/unit9/vl-nct-adding-nct-step-down-dn.svg)

Figura 28.3.4.
     4. Add a chromatic passing tone 

![](external/images/unit9/vl-nct-adding-nct-step-down-chro-p.svg)

Figura 28.3.5.
     5. Add an anticipation 

![](external/images/unit9/vl-nct-adding-nct-step-down-ant.svg)

Figura 28.3.6.

  3. Step up:

     1. Add a chromatic passing tone 

![](external/images/unit9/vl-nct-adding-nct-step-up-chro-p.svg)

Figura 28.3.7.
     2. Add an anticipation 

![](external/images/unit9/vl-nct-adding-nct-step-up-ant.svg)

Figura 28.3.8.
     3. Add an appoggiatura by leaping one step beyond the nota of resolution, placing the appogiatura on the beat, displacing the nota of resolution to occur after the beat 

![](external/images/unit9/vl-nct-adding-nct-step-up-app.svg)

Figura 28.3.9.
     4. Add a double neighbor 

![](external/images/unit9/vl-nct-adding-nct-step-up-dn.svg)

Figura 28.3.10.
     5. Add retardation by delaying resolution of the nota (rarely used) 

![](external/images/unit9/vl-nct-adding-nct-step-up-ret.svg)

Figura 28.3.11.

  4. Leap of 3rd:

     1. Fill in the 3rd with a passing tone 

![](external/images/unit9/vl-nct-adding-nct-3rd-down-p.svg)

Figura 28.3.12.
     2. Add an appoggiatura by leaping one step beyond the nota of resolution, placing the appogiatura on the beat, displacing the nota of resolution to occur after the beat 

![](external/images/unit9/vl-nct-adding-nct-3rd-up-app.svg)

Figura 28.3.13.

  5. Leaps of 4th or larger: do not embellish (for now)

Notice that, when non-acorde tones are added to different voices on the same beat, consonant intervalos (3rds, 5ths, 6ths, or 8ves) are made. If consonant intervalos can’t be made, the voices are staggered rhythmically (as occurs on beats 3 and 4 in compasso one of the example above).
