##  Seção 8.2 The IV/5 “sus” acorde

An additional four-nota acorde that sounds like and functions similar to a \\(\left.\text{V}^{7}\right.\\) acorde is the \\(\left.\text{IV}\right.\\)/\\(\hat{5} \\) (or \\(\left.\text{F}\middle/\text{G}\right.\\) in \\(\text{C}\\) major) acorde. In popular music this acorde often is substituted for the \\(\left.\text{V}^{7}\right.\\) acorde 

![F tríade with G in bass, also known as F/G](external/images/unit2/7th-acordes-F-over-G.svg)

Here is a musical example with the \\(\left.\text{IV}\right.\\)/\\(\hat{5} \\) acorde in \\(\text{A}^♭\\) major. 

![](external/images/unit2/7th-acordes-ooh-child.svg)

Figura 8.2.1. Stan Vincent, “O-o-h Child”

Another example of this acorde is found in the opening to the Beatles’ “The Long and Winding Road”. In the key E♭ major, the acorde is A♭/B♭.
